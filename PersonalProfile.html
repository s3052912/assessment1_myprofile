Learning Style Test – How to Study

URL: https://www.how-to-study.com/learning-style-assessment/

According to the How-to-Study learning style test, my Learning Style is a Tactile/Kinesthetic Learner.

Tactile/Kinesthetic learners learn best when they can use their sense of touch and can move about. Being a Tactile/Kinesthetic learner, the following suggestions that can help me to succeed in school to the best of my ability.

  • Be physically active while you study. Rather than just sit at your desk, occasionally walk back and forth with your textbook or notes as you read the information out loud.
  • To decrease your fidgeting as you study, listen to music, preferably baroque music. However, discontinue this if you find the music to be distracting.
  • Make extensive use of a computer and the Internet. Actively touching the keyboard will keep your mind active.
  • Take extensive written notes in class. Edit and type them later.
  • Study in short blocks of time with frequent but short breaks.
  • Do something physical as you study such as tapping a pencil or squeezing a stress ball.
  • Use your finger as a guide while reading.
  • Act out things you have to learn whenever possible.
  • Construct models of things you have to learn whenever possible.
  • If you find it difficult to sit at a desk when studying, trying lying on your stomach or back.
  • When trying to remember information, close your eyes and "write" the information in the air. Picture the information in your mind as you do so.
  • Use concrete objects to help you understand math concepts.
  • When trying to learn the spelling of a difficult word, arrange letter blocks to spell the word.

Summary

The results of the tests tell me that I lean slightly more towards being an introvert, which means in a group team setting I may at times hold back from speaking up and let others do more of the talking. I think this is a trait that I can improve on and spend more time in team settings to find more comfort in speaking up.

The tests also tell me that I’m a Tactile learner, which I agree with as I always learn better after actually doing something. In a team setting, I would prefer to engage with others in a practical hands-on way. This may be through screen sharing, where we can see each other working in real-time or have a visual component to it rather than just straight verbal communication.

My creativity levels are right in the middle, with room to improve. When forming a team I should look for a good balance of personalities, with a team that I can openly share ideas and collaborate with. Just enough extroverts to provide productive feedback for my ideas, but not too many extroverts that may dominate team meetings.
